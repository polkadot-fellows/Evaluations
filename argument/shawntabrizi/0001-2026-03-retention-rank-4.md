## Argument

I have been a member of the Polkadot Technical Fellowship since its seeding. During the first years of the fellowship, I was quite active as both a core developer and fellowship member.

Over the past year, I stepped back from day-to-day core development, but I remained active in the Polkadot community, mostly through education and advocacy. I have taught as a lead instructor at every Polkadot Blockchain Academy, most recently PBA 7 in Bali (Aug-Sep 2025), and I am signed up to teach at PBA Lisbon (Apr 2026). A lot of these students ended up joining the fellowship or working at Parity and other Polkadot teams. I have personally inducted 24 members into the fellowship on-chain, 9 of which were during this reporting period.

I also gave presentations on the fellowship, JAM, PolkaVM, and the Polkadot Cloud/Hub at various conferences during this period.

More recently, I have started returning to active development. I am building [Open Auto Battler](https://github.com/shawntabrizi/open-auto-battler), an on-chain game built with the Polkadot SDK, and have been doing code review and tooling work again. I hope to focus some time on tooling specifically bridging the Polkadot SDK and AI tooling.
